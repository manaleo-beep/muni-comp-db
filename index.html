Manual record entry

Manual entry tips

Use the municipality's exact working title in Job title, then map it to a standardized Mapped job title for cross-town benchmarking.

Example: one town may use Deputy Tax Collector, another may use Deputy Town Clerk / Assistant Tax Collector. Both can map to the same mapped job title for compensation analysis.

When duties differ enough to affect classification, create a new mapped job title rather than forcing a match.

Accepted import structure

The app accepts Excel or CSV uploads. For a multi-sheet workbook, it looks for sheets named Jobs, Descriptions, Role_Families, and Title_Aliases. At minimum, a Jobs sheet should include:

state, municipality, department, category, job_title, role_family_id, role_family_name, grade, union_status, pay_frequency, minimum_pay, midpoint_pay, maximum_pay, current_pay, annual_hours, fte, effective_date, created_as_of, valid_year, source_type, source_document, source_url, notes

The companion workbook delivered with this app already includes a working template, manual-entry tab, title-alias tab, and benchmark formulas.

Seeded with standardized NH, Vermont, and Maine public examples including uploaded workbook data, compensation studies, non-union pay plans, wage scales, benchmark reports, and union-contract compensation tables. Uploaded data is processed locally in your browser.
